Financial Checklist for Studying in Germany (Nepali Students)
Use this checklist to ensure you have sufficient funds and documentation for your German National Visa for Study Purposes application and first year of study.

PART 1: MAJOR EXPENSES (First Year)
Estimate in EUR. Use the high-end for safe planning.

1. Tuition & University Fees
Tuition Fees: €0 - €3,000 *(Most public unis: €0; Baden-Württemberg charges €1,500/semester for non-EU)*

Semester Contribution (Mandatory): €150 - €350 per semester (covers transport pass, admin, student services)

2. Living Expenses (12 Months)
Housing (Rent): €4,200 - €9,600 (WG in small city vs. studio in Munich)

Food/Groceries: €2,400 - €3,600

Health Insurance (Mandatory): €1,200 - €1,400 (public insurance, e.g., TK, AOK)

Books & Supplies: €200 - €500

Personal/Miscellaneous: €1,800 - €3,600 (phone, leisure, clothing)

Transportation: Covered by semester ticket in most cities

3. Visa & Blocked Account
Blocked Account (Sperrkonto) Minimum: €11,208 for one year (mandatory for visa)

Visa Application Fee: €75

4. Travel & Initial Setup
Flight to Germany (One-way): €600 - €1,200

Initial Settling-in Costs: €1,000 - €2,000 (deposit, bedding, kitchen essentials)

PART 2: VISA & PRE-DEPARTURE COSTS (NPR)
Costs paid in Nepal before departure.

5. Testing & Applications
TestDaF/Goethe German Exam Fee: NPR 25,000 - 35,000 (for German-taught programs)

IELTS/TOEFL Exam Fee: NPR 26,000 - 30,000 (for English-taught programs)

TestAS Exam Fee: ~€80 (~NPR 11,500) (recommended for many bachelor's programs)

uni-assist Application Fee: €75 for first uni, €30 for each additional (if applicable)

6. Visa & Documentation
German Visa Application Fee: €75 (~NPR 11,000)

Blocked Account Setup Fee: €50 - €150 (varies by provider, e.g., Fintiba, Expatrio)

Medical Check-up: Not typically required for student visa

Police Clearance Certificate: NPR 2,000 - 3,000

Documentation & Notarization/Translation: NPR 10,000 - 20,000 (for German translations of certificates)

PART 3: PROOF OF FUNDS FOR VISA APPLICATION
Documents to provide as evidence of financial capacity.

7. Primary Evidence (Mandatory)
Proof of Blocked Account (Sperrkonto) with €11,208 for one year

University Admission Letter (Zulassungsbescheid)

Proof of Health Insurance valid from date of entry

8. Supporting Documents
Affidavit of Sponsorship (Verpflichtungserklärung) if sponsored by someone in Germany

Parents'/Sponsor's Bank Statements & Income Proof (even with blocked account)

Scholarship Award Letter (e.g., from DAAD) if applicable

PART 4: FUNDING SOURCES CHECKLIST
How you will cover the costs.

9. Personal/Family Funds
Savings in Nepalese bank for blocked account and initial costs

Fixed deposits (as proof of additional funds)

10. Blocked Account Setup
Choose a provider: Fintiba, Expatrio, Coracle, Deutsche Bank (via partner)

Transfer €11,208 + setup fee to the blocked account

Receive confirmation document for visa application

11. Scholarships & Part-Time Work
DAAD Scholarships: Fully funded for excellent students (highly competitive)

Deutschlandstipendium: €300/month (apply after arrival)

University-specific scholarships

Part-time Work Rights: 120 full days or 240 half days per year at €12+/hour

PART 5: POST-ARRIVAL FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT
After reaching Germany.

12. Immediate Actions
Register at Resident’s Registration Office (Einwohnermeldeamt) within 2 weeks

Open a German current account (Girokonto) for monthly payouts from blocked account

Confirm health insurance activation

Apply for Residence Permit (Aufenthaltstitel) at Foreigners’ Office

13. Ongoing Management
Receive monthly payout (€934) from blocked account to your German bank account

Track monthly budget carefully (rent is largest expense)

Look for HiWi (student assistant) jobs at university after first semester

Understand tax basics (income tax on earnings above €10,908/year)

Final Verification:

Blocked account is set up with at least €11,208.

All university and visa documents are collected and translated.

You have extra funds (€2,000-€3,000) for initial costs beyond the blocked account.

You understand the monthly budget based on the €934 payout.

Remember: The German embassy/consulate is very strict about the blocked account proof. It is the most critical document for your visa approval.
