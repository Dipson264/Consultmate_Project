Financial Checklist for Studying in the USA (Nepali Students)
Use this checklist to ensure you have sufficient funds and documentation for your U.S. student visa (F-1) application and first year of study.

PART 1: MAJOR EXPENSES (First Year)
Estimate in USD. Use the high-end for safe planning.

1. Tuition & University Fees
Tuition Fees: $25,000 - $60,000+ *(Verify exact amount on your I-20)*

University Fees (Mandatory): $500 - $2,000 (health, activity, tech fees)

Orientation Fee: $200 - $500 (if applicable)

2. Living Expenses (9-12 Months)
Housing (Rent): $8,000 - $20,000

Food/Groceries: $3,000 - $6,000

Utilities/Internet: $1,200 - $2,400

Books & Supplies: $800 - $1,500

Personal/Miscellaneous: $2,000 - $4,000

Transportation (Local): $600 - $1,500

3. Insurance & Healthcare
Health Insurance (Mandatory): $1,500 - $3,000 (university plan)

Medical Emergency Fund: $1,000 - $2,000 (recommended)

4. Travel & Settlement
Flight to USA (One-way): $1,000 - $2,000

Initial Settling-in Costs: $1,000 - $2,000 (deposit, bedding, kitchen essentials)

PART 2: VISA & PRE-DEPARTURE COSTS (NPR)
Costs paid in Nepal before departure.

5. Testing & Applications
TOEFL/IELTS Exam Fee: NPR 26,000 - 30,000

SAT/ACT Exam Fee: NPR 25,000 - 35,000

GRE/GMAT Exam Fee: NPR 30,000 - 35,000

University Application Fees: $50 - $100 per university

6. Visa & Documentation
SEVIS I-901 Fee: $350 (mandatory, paid online)

US Visa Application Fee (MRV): $185 (paid at bank)

VFS/Offsite Facilitation Fee: NPR 4,500 (if applicable)

Documentation & Notarization: NPR 5,000 - 10,000

Medical Check-up: NPR 10,000 - 15,000 (if required)

PART 3: PROOF OF FUNDS FOR VISA INTERVIEW
Documents to show Consular Officer.

7. Primary Evidence (Required)
I-20 Form from university with total cost of attendance

Bank Statements (last 6 months) showing sufficient balance

Education Loan Sanction Letter (if applicable)

Scholarship/Fellowship Award Letter (if applicable)

PART 4: FUNDING SOURCES CHECKLIST
How you will cover the costs.

9. Personal/Family Funds
Savings in Nepalese bank (convertible to USD)

Fixed deposits (can be liquidated)

Property documents (as proof of strong ties, not primary funds)

10. Education Loan
Research banks: Nabil, NIC Asia, Global IME, Prabhu

Compare: Interest rate, margin, processing fee, moratorium period

Required: Collateral (property), co-signer, admission proof, I-20

Loan amount: Typically 80-90% of I-20 total, up to NPR 50 lakhs

11. Scholarships & Assistantships
University Merit Scholarships: Applied for with admission

TA/RA Positions: For graduate students, provides stipend + tuition waiver

External Scholarships: Fulbright, Rotary, private foundations

Part-time On-campus Job: Up to 20 hours/week at $12-$18/hour

PART 5: POST-ARRIVAL FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT
After reaching the USA.

12. Immediate Actions
Open US bank account (requires passport, I-20, local address)

Get Social Security Number (SSN) if working on-campus

Set up budgeting app/tracker (Mint, YNAB)

Understand tax obligations (may need to file even without income)

13. Ongoing Management
Track monthly spending (rent, food, phone, transportation)

Build credit history responsibly (secured credit card)

Explore paid internships (CPT) after 1 year for relevant field experience

Plan for OPT (post-graduation work) and potential H-1B visa costs

Final Verification:

All amounts match or exceed the "Total Cost" on your I-20 form

Funds are liquid or easily accessible

Documentation is organized and translated (if in Nepali)

You can confidently explain your financial plan to the visa officer

Remember: The visa officer must be convinced you can cover the entire first year without unauthorized work and have credible funding sources for subsequent years.
