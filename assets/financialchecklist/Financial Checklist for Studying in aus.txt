Financial Checklist for Studying in Australia (Nepali Students)
Use this checklist to ensure you have sufficient funds and documentation for your Australian student visa (subclass 500) application and first year of study.

PART 1: MAJOR EXPENSES (First Year)
Estimate in AUD. Use the high-end for safe planning.

1. Tuition & University Fees
Tuition Fees: $24,000 - $50,000+ (Arts/Business at regional uni vs. Medicine at Group of Eight)

University Services & Amenities Fee (SSAF): $0 - $350 (varies by state/uni)

Orientation/Enrolment Fee: $0 - $300 (if applicable)

2. Living Expenses (12 Months)
Housing (Rent): $9,000 - $22,000 (shared regional vs. private studio in Sydney/Melbourne)

Food/Groceries: $5,000 - $9,000

Utilities/Internet: $1,200 - $2,400

Books & Supplies: $500 - $1,500

Personal/Miscellaneous: $3,000 - $6,000

Transportation (Local): $800 - $2,000

3. Insurance & Healthcare
Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) - Mandatory: $600 - $800 for 12 months (single policy)

4. Travel & Settlement
Flight to Australia (One-way): $1,200 - $2,500

Initial Settling-in Costs: $1,500 - $3,000 (rental bond, basic furnishings, kitchenware)

PART 2: VISA & PRE-DEPARTURE COSTS (NPR)
Costs paid in Nepal before departure.

5. Testing & Applications
IELTS/TOEFL/PTE Exam Fee: NPR 26,000 - 31,000

University Application Fees: $50 - $150 per university

6. Visa & Documentation
Student Visa Application Fee (Subclass 500): AUD $710 (~NPR 63,000)

Biometrics Collection Fee (if applicable): NPR 4,500

Medical Check-up: NPR 15,000 - 25,000 (if requested by immigration)

Police Clearance Certificate: NPR 2,000 - 3,000

Documentation & Notarization: NPR 5,000 - 10,000

PART 3: PROOF OF FUNDS FOR VISA APPLICATION
Documents to provide as evidence of financial capacity.

7. Primary Evidence (Required)
Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from your Australian institution

Evidence of Funds for 12 Months:

Option A: Bank statements (last 3-6 months) showing sufficient balance

Option B: Evidence of an approved education loan

Option C: Official scholarship/sponsorship documents

Proof you meet the annual living cost requirement: AUD $24,505 (2024 rate)

8. Supporting Documents
Income Proof of Sponsor/Parents (salary slips, tax returns, business documents)

Affidavit of Support (if sponsored by parents/relative)

Property Valuation Reports (as evidence of strong financial background)

Fixed Deposit Certificates with maturity dates

PART 4: FUNDING SOURCES CHECKLIST
How you will cover the costs.

9. Personal/Family Funds
Savings in Nepalese bank (convertible to AUD)

Fixed deposits (can be liquidated)

Property documents (as proof of strong ties/financial standing)

10. Education Loan
Research banks: Nabil, NIC Asia, Global IME, Prabhu, Himalayan Bank

Compare: Interest rate, margin, processing fee, moratorium period

Required: Collateral (property), co-signer, admission proof, CoE

Loan amount: Typically 80-90% of total costs, up to NPR 50-60 lakhs

11. Scholarships & Part-Time Work
University Merit Scholarships: Applied for with admission (e.g., Vice-Chancellor's Scholarship)

Government Scholarships: Australia Awards (competitive, fully funded)

Destination Australia Scholarships: For regional campus study

Part-time Work Rights: Up to 48 hours/fortnight during semester at $23.23+/hour (minimum wage)

PART 5: POST-ARRIVAL FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT
After reaching Australia.

12. Immediate Actions
Open Australian bank account (requires passport, CoE, local address)

Apply for Tax File Number (TFN) if planning to work

Set up budgeting tracker

Activate OSHC policy

13. Ongoing Management
Track monthly spending (rent, food, phone, transport)

Understand Australian tax system (may need to file a return)

Explore paid internships related to your field

Plan for Temporary Graduate Visa (subclass 485) costs after graduation

Final Verification:

All amounts meet or exceed the costs listed on your CoE and the living cost requirement.

Funds are liquid or accessible.

All financial documents are clear, translated if necessary, and organized.

You can explain your financial plan clearly if contacted by the Department of Home Affairs.

Remember: The Department of Home Affairs must be convinced you can genuinely afford your studies and living costs for the duration of your stay without relying on work that breaches your visa conditions.
